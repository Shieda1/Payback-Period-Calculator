What is the payback period?
payback period is the time needed to recover the cost of the investment. simply it is the time an investment takes to reach the break even point. How to calculate payback period?
To calculate payback period you should use the following formula:
payback period = cost of investment / average annual cash flow
example: company x investes $600,000 in a product that will save the company $120,000 each year, calculate the payback period.
using the payback period formula the answer will be:
payback period = cost of investment / average annual cash flow
payback period = $600,000 / $120,000
payback period = 5 years for this investment to arrive at the pay back period
Why use payback period?
Investors and corporations use the payback period to calculate investments returns. And it also helps determine how long it takes to recover the initial costs of an investment.
